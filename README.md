$\LARGE \color{#FFD166}{\textsf{DESCRIPTION}}$

$\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Unilearn}}$ is an Android $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{space\ sandbox\ game}}$ built in $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Godot}}$, where the universe becomes something you can $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{play\ with,\ break,\ rebuild,\ learn\ from,\ and\ command}}$.

Create unstable systems, throw planets into orbit, collect and generate $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Planet\ Cards}}$, trigger black-hole disasters, complete quizzes, trade with nearby players, unlock achievements, and experiment with a galaxy that actually reacts.

This is not a static astronomy app.  
It is a $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{playable\ universe}}$.

---

$\LARGE \color{#FFD166}{\textsf{FEATURES}}$

• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Universe\ sandbox}}$ with planets, moons, stars, brown dwarfs, white dwarfs, black holes, white holes, satellites, and custom systems  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Galaxy\ Simulator}}$ with stable orbits, collisions, trails, binary systems, gravity, body evolution, accretion disks, attraction, repulsion, and large-scale cosmic events  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Planet\ Cards}}$ with scientific data, visual presets, search and filters, levels, XP, statistics, badges, quizzes, and collectible progression  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{AI-generated\ celestial\ bodies}}$ with validated measurements, descriptions, learning content, gameplay data, and complete visual identities  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Card\ sticker\ export}}$ through an interactive hold-to-render animation for saving Planet Cards as shareable images  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{80\ staged\ achievements}}$ across eight categories, with Bronze, Silver, Gold, hidden objectives, rare events, progress tracking, and animated unlock feedback  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{BLE\ nearby\ multiplayer}}$ with direct player discovery, request flows, Planet Card trading, and temporary shared-universe synchronization  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Authenticated\ offline\ mode}}$ with locally cached Planet Cards, live connectivity feedback, protected online-only actions, and achievement synchronization after reconnecting  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Apollo\ AI}}$ for local voice commands, navigation, simulator controls, generated cards, quizzes, linked actions, and guided interaction  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Interactive\ Apollo\ tutorial}}$ that demonstrates the application in under a minute and can be replayed by asking how to use the app  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Firebase\ synchronization}}$ for accounts, cards, progression, achievements, saved galaxies, player profiles, and multiplayer coordination  
• $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Custom\ Android\ plugins}}$, pixel-style celestial bodies, shaders, reactive sound design, smooth mobile animations, and a high-contrast space interface

$\LARGE \color{#FFD166}{\textsf{GAMEPLAY}}$

Unilearn is built around $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{experimentation,\ discovery,\ and\ controlled\ chaos}}$.

Build a system, add celestial bodies, drag and throw them through space, then watch them $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{orbit,\ drift,\ collide,\ transform,\ collapse}}$, or become something entirely new. Adjust the simulation through the Galaxy Console, create binary systems, evolve stars, form singularities, and test how far the universe can be pushed.

Outside the simulator, search and filter your collection, generate new Planet Cards, level them through quizzes, export them as stickers, trade them with nearby players, synchronize temporary universes, and chase achievements built around both scientific exploration and cosmic nonsense.

Your objective is simple: $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{explore,\ discover,\ experiment,\ and\ unlock\ everything}}$.

$\LARGE \color{#FFD166}{\textsf{CORE\ SYSTEMS}}$

<h3><ins>Apollo AI</ins></h3>

$\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Apollo}}$ is Unilearn's in-game AI companion. A custom Android speech plugin recognizes local commands for navigation, settings, simulation actions, Planet Card generation, and linked requests. Apollo reacts through listening, thinking, speaking, and idle states, and can launch the complete guided tutorial through commands such as `How to use the app?`.

<h3><ins>Planet Cards</ins></h3>

$\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Planet\ Cards}}$ are collectible cosmic profiles containing scientific measurements, descriptions, learning prompts, visual identities, progression data, statistics, badges, and quizzes. Cards can be searched, filtered, generated, levelled, added to the simulator, traded, or rendered as shareable sticker images.

<h3><ins>Galaxy Simulator</ins></h3>

The $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Galaxy\ Simulator}}$ is the main playground. It supports stable orbit initialization, satellites, anchors, binaries, collisions, evolutionary transformations, black-hole attraction, white-hole repulsion, accretion disks, trails, camera controls, and special end-state sequences.

<h3><ins>Galaxy Console</ins></h3>

The Galaxy Console exposes simulation data, behavior controls, quick commands, and live results. Players can adjust gravity, time, trails, orbital behavior, camera settings, and other parameters while the active universe reacts immediately.

<h3><ins>Achievements</ins></h3>

The achievement system contains $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{80\ multi-stage\ objectives}}$ covering added bodies, planetary collisions, stellar collisions, singularities, stat mastery, Apollo, unstable systems, and card collection. Normal and rare achievements progress through Bronze, Silver, and Gold stages.

<h3><ins>Nearby Multiplayer</ins></h3>

$\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Multiplayer}}$ uses Bluetooth Low Energy to discover nearby Android players. Players can exchange Planet Cards or enter temporary shared-universe sessions where body additions, removals, and Galaxy Console changes are synchronized while each device keeps responsive local physics.

<h3><ins>Offline Mode</ins></h3>

$\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{Authenticated\ players}}$ can open their $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{locally\ cached\ Planet\ Card\ collection}}$ and continue supported activities without internet access. Unavailable actions clearly enter a $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{responsive\ offline\ state}}$, repeated backend attempts stop, and locally earned $\color{#FFD166}{\textsf{achievement\ progress}}$ synchronizes automatically when connectivity returns.

$\LARGE \color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{TECH\ STACK}}$

• $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{Godot\ 4.6}}$ + $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{GDScript}}$ for the Android application, UI, simulation, cards, progression, achievements, tutorial, and multiplayer flows  
• $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{GDShader}}$ and $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{MultiMesh}}$ rendering for animated space, celestial previews, stars, card effects, and optimized visual systems  
• $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{Java}}$ with $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{ApolloSpeechPlugin}}$ for native Android speech recognition and local intent parsing  
• $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{Kotlin}}$ with $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{UnilearnBlePlugin}}$ for BLE advertising, scanning, peer discovery, and Android permission handling  
• $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{Native\ Android\ connectivity\ monitoring}}$ for responsive online/offline state changes without repeated HTTP connectivity probes  
• $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{Firebase\ Authentication}}$ + $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{Firestore}}$ for accounts, Planet Cards, achievements, galaxies, profiles, and multiplayer coordination  
• $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{Node.js\ +\ Express}}$ backend for player initialization, validated AI content generation, quizzes, card progression, and protected data operations  
• $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{OpenAI-powered\ generation}}$ for new Planet Card data, learning content, visual configuration, and card-based quizzes

$\LARGE \color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{BACKEND\ AND\ DATA}}$

Unilearn uses a $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{backend-supported\ architecture}}$ for $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{AI\ generation}}$, $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{progression}}$, $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{multiplayer}}$, and $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{cloud\ synchronization}}$.

```txt
users/{uid}
├── planetCards/{cardId}
├── achievements/state
└── multiplayer and profile data
```

$\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{Planet\ Cards}}$ and $\color{#8FD8FF}{\textsf{achievement\ state}}$ are stored separately so collection data can be restored independently.
